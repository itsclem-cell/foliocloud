Accessibility Statement

AccessibleMe is committed to making this website accessible to everyone. This statement explains our commitment, what we've done, what we know still needs doing, and how to contact us if you experience any barriers.

Last reviewed: March 2026

✓ This site is designed to conform to WCAG 2.2 Level AA. An independent audit is planned and will be documented here when complete. We are not aware of any part of this site that fails to meet the standard at the time of publication.

Our commitment

Accessibility is not an afterthought here — it is the foundation of everything we build and recommend. This website is intentionally built to the same standard we deliver for clients, and we treat it as a live demonstration of what good looks like.

We are committed to continuous improvement and to ensuring that as many people as possible can use this site — regardless of disability, neurodivergence, or mental health need.

Technical information

This website is built using semantic HTML5, with CSS and vanilla JavaScript. We do not use frameworks that generate inaccessible markup. All JavaScript is progressive enhancement — the site is functional without JS enabled.

We have tested this site using:

  • NVDA screen reader with Firefox (Windows)
  • VoiceOver with Safari (macOS and iOS)
  • TalkBack with Chrome (Android)
  • Keyboard-only navigation across all pages
  • axe DevTools browser extension
  • Colour Contrast Analyser across all colour modes
  • Browser zoom to 200% and 400%

Known issues and limitations

We are not aware of any current issues that prevent full access to the site's content or functions. However:

  • An independent WCAG 2.2 Level AA audit is planned but not yet completed. This statement will be updated when audit results are available.
  • Third-party embeds (e.g. video platforms or booking tools) may not be fully within our control. We choose third-party tools with accessibility in mind wherever possible.

How to report a problem

If you experience any difficulty accessing content or functionality on this site, please tell us. We take all accessibility reports seriously and will investigate promptly.

You can contact us:

  • By email: hello@accessibleme.solutions — please include the page URL and a description of the problem
  • By form: Use our contact form

We aim to respond within 5 working days.

Enforcement

If you are not satisfied with our response, you can contact the Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS), who provide free advice and support on discrimination and human rights issues.
